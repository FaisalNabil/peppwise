Technical Architecture

How the Technical Stack Works

Two specialised systems, two distinct document flows. Understanding the architecture is the first step to implementing it correctly — and connecting them across borders.

Malaysia e-Invoice
Document Lifecycle
LHDN MyInvois · IRBM Validation
1
Document Created in ERP / System

Invoice, Credit Note, Debit Note, Refund Note, or Self-Billed Invoice generated with all mandatory IRBM fields: Supplier TIN, Buyer TIN, MSIC code, SST registration number, and line-item tax category codes.

2
Two Paths
Submitted to LHDN

Via the LHDN API (programmatic, ERP/middleware integration) or the MyInvois Portal (manual, SME-friendly). Document is signed with an X.509 digital certificate before transmission.

3
Real-Time
Validated by LHDN — UUID & QR Assigned

LHDN validates structure, TIN references, tax calculations, and MSIC codes in real time. On success, a UUID (unique invoice identifier) and QR code are generated and returned. This timestamp starts the 72-hour windows.

4
72 Hours
Buyer Notified — Rejection Window

Buyer receives the validated invoice and has 72 hours from the LHDN validation timestamp to formally reject it. The seller can also cancel within the same window.

5
Validated Invoice Archived

If not rejected or cancelled, the invoice is confirmed. Both parties must retain the validated document — including UUID and QR code — for the IRBM-stipulated archival period. The QR code links to the LHDN verification portal.

Singapore InvoiceNow / Peppol
4-Corner → 5-Corner Flow
IMDA Peppol Network · IRAS C5 Integration
C1
Sender (Malaysian or SG Company)

The originating business generates the invoice in Peppol BIS Billing 3.0 format with Singapore-specific extensions — including GST treatment codes and PayNow payment reference where applicable.

C2
Sender's Peppol Access Point

The sender's IMDA-certified Access Point transmits the document over the Peppol network using the AS4 messaging protocol. The sender's Peppol ID (registered in SML/SMP) determines the routing path.

C3
Receiver's Peppol Access Point

The receiver's Access Point retrieves the document. For cross-border MY↔SG trade, Malaysia's MDEC-designated interoperability layer routes documents between the two national Peppol environments.

C4
Receiver (Trading Partner)

The buyer receives the structured invoice directly into their ERP or accounting system. No email, no PDF re-keying. Real-time B2B document exchange with a complete audit trail.

C5
Singapore-Pioneer
IRAS — Automated GST Submission

The 5th corner, pioneered by Singapore. Access Points forward GST-relevant invoice data directly to IRAS (Inland Revenue Authority of Singapore) for automated tax reporting — eliminating manual GST F5/F7 reconciliation for qualifying transactions.
